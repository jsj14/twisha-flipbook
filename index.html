💔 For Twisha

This one is for Twisha.

Twisha Sharma was 31 years old. A model. Educated. She had a whole life ahead of her.

She married Samarth Singh in December 2025 and moved to Bhopal to live with his family. Five months later, she was found dead.

Her family says she was harassed for dowry. That she lost 15 kilos after the wedding. That she sent messages saying she was scared and wanted to leave.

Her mother-in-law, Giribala Singh, was a former district judge.

A woman who spent her career in courtrooms. Who knew the law better than most people ever will. And yet — the CBI arrested them both. The husband, a lawyer. The mother-in-law, a judge.

📌 The point of this book
Education does NOT protect you. Status does NOT protect you. Knowing your rights might. This book exists so you know them before you need them.

For her.

1
🚫 Myth 1

Your family has to give dowry.

✅ Reality check
Dowry is ILLEGAL. Giving it. Taking it. Demanding it. All of it is a criminal offence punishable by 5+ years in prison.

This has been the law since 1961. Over sixty years. And yet here we are.

"But everyone does it." Everyone also speeds. It is still ILLEGAL. "But we are calling it a gift." Does NOT matter. If it is expected because of the marriage — it is dowry.

Twisha's mother-in-law was a judge. She allegedly broke this law anyway. If that tells you anything, it is this: the law only protects you if you know it and use it.

⚖️ The actual law
Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961 — Section 3: giving OR taking dowry = minimum 5 years jail + fine.

IPC Section 498A: harassment for dowry is a separate criminal offence. Police MUST register the case. They CANNOT refuse.
2
🎁 Myth 2

Gifts demanded after the wedding are fine. That is not really dowry.

✅ Reality check
It IS still dowry. The law covers demands made before, during, AND after the wedding. There is no loophole.

A very common move: do NOT ask before the wedding. Ask after. Call it a gift. Call it help. Call it a contribution. The law sees through it.

The Dowry Prohibition Act defines dowry as anything given "in connection with the marriage" — NOT just at the wedding. A demand six months later is ILLEGAL if it is connected to the marriage.

📋 What to do
If your in-laws are demanding money, jewellery, a car, land, or anything else after the wedding — document it. Screenshot messages. Save voice notes. Keep records. Then speak to a lawyer.

Asking is already the crime. You do NOT have to hand anything over.
3
😤 Myth 3

You have to put up with taunts and comparisons.

✅ Reality check
NO. Taunts, insults, and constant comparisons are mental cruelty. The Kerala High Court has ruled this is grounds for divorce.

Being told you are not good enough. Being compared to his ex, his colleague, his cousin's wife. Being humiliated in front of family. Being made to feel worthless — over and over. This is NOT "just how marriages work." This is cruelty.

You are NOT too sensitive. You are NOT overreacting. If someone is breaking you down every single day, that is NOT a problem you need to fix. That is cruelty.

⚖️ The actual law
Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 — Section 13(1)(ia): cruelty, including mental cruelty, is a ground for divorce. You do NOT need a hospital report. Sustained verbal abuse, humiliation, and demeaning comparisons all count.

Supreme Court confirmed this in 2007. The Kerala High Court has applied it specifically to taunts and comparisons.
4
💸 Myth 4

Your salary belongs to the household. He gets a say.

✅ Reality check
He does NOT. Your money is yours. A married woman has complete, independent financial rights in India.

Nobody — NOT your husband, NOT your in-laws — has a legal right to your salary. You can open a bank account, buy property, and start a business without asking anyone.

If someone is controlling your money through fear, taking your salary by force, or stopping you from accessing your own account — that is ILLEGAL. It is economic abuse. And it is domestic violence under Indian law.

⚖️ The actual law
Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005 — Section 3: economic abuse — controlling your money, stopping you from working, taking your earnings — is legally DOMESTIC VIOLENCE. It is NOT just "a marriage problem." You can file a complaint and get a protection order.
5
🏠 Myth 5

You have to live with his parents.

✅ Reality check
You do NOT. There is no law in India that forces a wife to live with her in-laws. Not one.

You and your husband decide where you live — together. That is it. If living with in-laws is making your life miserable and you are being pressured to stay, that pressure itself can be a form of domestic violence.

Also: if his family tries to throw you out of the home you share with your husband, they CANNOT legally do that. You have the right to stay.

⚖️ The actual law
Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005 — Section 17: you have the right to stay in the shared home, even if your name is NOT on the lease or the property papers. Nobody can remove you from it without a court order.
6
📛 Myth 6

You have to change your name.

✅ Reality check
No Indian law says you have to change your name after marriage. It is 100% YOUR CHOICE.

Nobody can force you to change your name on your Aadhaar, your passport, your degree certificate, or anywhere else. You are NOT legally required to.

Keep your name. Change it. Hyphenate it. Use different names in different places. All fine. All legal.

📌 Why this matters
The pressure to change your name is about identity — making you feel like the person you were before marriage no longer exists. The law does NOT ask this of you. Anyone who says it is "required" is confusing custom with law.
7
🏡 Myth 7

If you die without a will, your property goes back to your parents.

✅ Reality check
NO. If you die without a will, your property goes to your HUSBAND first. If he is also gone, it goes to HIS parents — NOT yours. Your own parents are last in line.

This is the one that shocks people the most. A woman spends years earning, saving, building — and assumes that if something happens to her, it goes back to her family. Her mother. Her father. Her siblings.

Under the Hindu Succession Act, when a married woman dies without a will, the order is: first her children, then her husband, then her husband's heirs. Her own parents only inherit if she has no children, no husband, and no in-laws in the picture.

That gold you earned. That flat you bought. That savings account. Without a will, it could all go to people your own parents never chose — and there is NOTHING legally wrong with that. Which is exactly why you need a will.

✍️ Write a will. Now.
A will is the ONLY way to make sure your property goes where you want it to go. Without one, the law hands it to your husband's side by default.

You do NOT need to be rich. You do NOT need to be old. A basic will, signed and witnessed, is legally valid. Do it now — before you need someone else to.

Reference: Hindu Succession Act, 1956 — Section 15 & 16 (order of succession for a Hindu woman dying intestate).
8
👭 Myth 8

Your brother gets more of your parents' property than you.

✅ Reality check
NOT anymore. Since 2005, daughters have the EXACT same rights as sons to ancestral property. The Supreme Court confirmed this in 2020.

For generations, ancestral property passed through sons. Daughters got nothing or got whatever the family chose to give. That changed in 2005. Daughters get the same share as sons — NOT as a favour, as a legal right.

In 2020, the Supreme Court went further: this right exists from the day you were born. Even if your father died before 2005. Even if the land was divided before 2005. You still have a claim. It CANNOT be taken from you.

⚖️ Your right
If you are a Hindu woman and your family has ancestral property, you are entitled to the same share as your brothers. Marriage does NOT remove this right.

Reference: Hindu Succession (Amendment) Act, 2005 — Section 6; Vineeta Sharma v. Rakesh Sharma (2020)
9
📃 Myth 9

You need his permission to get a divorce.

✅ Reality check
You do NOT. You can file for divorce even if he refuses. The court decides — NOT him.

This myth keeps women stuck. "He will never agree, so I am trapped." You are NOT trapped.

There are two types of divorce in India. Mutual consent (both agree) and contested divorce (you file, the court decides). If he will NOT agree, you file a contested divorce. Legal grounds include: cruelty, desertion for two or more years, adultery. Mental cruelty — taunts, humiliation, abuse — counts.

⚖️ The actual law
Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 — Section 13: you can file for divorce without your husband's consent. If the court finds your grounds are valid, divorce WILL be granted. His refusal is NOT a veto.
10
⚠️ Myth 10

The law protects you from everything inside a marriage.

✅ Reality check
On some things, the law is still catching up. Here is the honest truth — and where it does NOT yet go far enough.

Marital rape is NOT yet fully a criminal offence under the IPC. The Supreme Court is reviewing this. It is NOT here yet. Dowry laws exist but enforcement is uneven — Twisha's mother-in-law was a judge and allegedly still broke the law.

Imperfect does NOT mean powerless.

📞 What you can do right now
→ Non-consensual acts in marriage: raise it as sexual domestic violence under PWDVA, 2005.
→ Any abuse: document everything. Dates, messages, voice notes, photos.
→ Free legal help: District Legal Services Authority (DLSA) — free of charge.
→ In danger now: call 112 (emergency) or 181 (women's helpline).
⭐ Bonus — Because Twisha was NOT the first.

Vismaya was a medical student. Her husband got 10 years in jail. Here is what convicted him.

📱 Digital evidence saved the case
Two days before she died, Vismaya sent her cousin photos of her injuries — bruises on her face, shoulder, and hands. She sent voice messages describing the abuse. Those screenshots and recordings became the spine of a 507-page chargesheet. Her husband Kiran Kumar was convicted and sentenced to 10 years in prison.

Vismaya V Nair was 22 years old. An Ayurveda medical student. Found dead at her in-laws' house in Kerala in June 2021 — less than a year after her wedding. Her family had given 100 sovereigns of gold, one acre of land, and a brand new car as dowry. Her husband wanted a different car model. He wanted cash. He kept demanding more.

She called her mother from the bathroom so he wouldn't hear. She told her mother he had hit her so badly her mouth was bleeding. Her mother told her to come back. Vismaya said she was worried about what people would say.

She stayed. She should NOT have had to.

⚖️ The laws that convicted him — and could protect YOU
IPC Section 498A — cruelty and dowry harassment by husband or in-laws. COGNISABLE offence. Police must act.

IPC Section 304B — dowry death. If a woman dies within 7 years of marriage and there is evidence of dowry harassment, the court presumes guilt. Minimum 7 years imprisonment.

IPC Section 306 — abetment of suicide. If someone's actions drove you to it, they are criminally liable.

📱 Your phone is evidence. Screenshots. Voice notes. Photos of injuries. WhatsApp messages. Save them somewhere safe. Send them to someone you trust. This is what put Kiran Kumar in prison.
